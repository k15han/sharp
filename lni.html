Of Love and Intimacy

by Kishan Chauhan

In deep sleepiness
shallow breathlessness
elusive briskness
obscure murkiness,
like raindrops, I dawdle,
that stick to the roof
before finally falling off.
While poetically articulating
the breathtaking wonders
of your lustrous anatomy
I dare not deduce it,
to touch is beyond
to embrace is further
to possess is hopeless.


Your whispery discourse
humming laughter,
your graceful proposals
meticulous nursing,
your drowsy blinks
rustling motions,
your sweaty fingers
amorous fondling
draws me to you,
to your lines and rays
your artistic arcs
and the silvery spheres,
your curvy columns
and the gap between.


What assumes thenceforth
like the crack of dawn,
besides gush of kisses
spilling over our places,
like the onset of spring,
besides poppies of passion
budding over our fields,
indeed is the flow of love
brimming into our bodies
and swashing us unconscious,
the sharp scent of intimacy
diffusing into our nerves,
and drifting us ultimately
toward our other halves.
