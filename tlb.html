The Last Breath

by Kishan Chauhan

Someday I shall be free
while the weather be calm,
scorching sun not so angry

Someday I shall be free,
free from the terms of love
from the conditions for peace

Someday I shall be free,
free from scolds and hatred
from this mental affliction
of the undergoing battle:
against inherited morals stands
what my conscience discerns

Someday I shall be free
as the ripples go dull,
roaring wind be serene

Someday I shall be free,
free from the grudges I bear
from the enmities I fear

Someday I shall be free,
free from opinions and suggestions
later that turn out to be decree
from the burden of expectations
bestowed upon my back,
my back like a broken twig
hangs weary and utterly weak

Someday I believe
freedom will be near,
as illness gets intense
and like age,
concern too lessens

Someday I believe
I will be free,
lying on my deathbed
as escapes the last breath

Someday conversely,
like the wavy waters
dazzling in the twilight glow,
can my distressed soul be lightened up with hope?
